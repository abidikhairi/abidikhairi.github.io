About My Research

The Linguistic Nature of Biology

Life speaks in a complex language of molecules, proteins, and genetic codes. My research explores the striking parallels between human language and biological information processing, developing computational frameworks to translate between these two fundamental languages of existence.

By applying natural language processing techniques to genomic sequences and protein structures, we're uncovering grammatical rules in DNA, semantic relationships between proteins, and even potential "dialects" in cellular communication.

Research Focus Areas

Genomic Linguistics

Developing grammatical models for DNA sequences that can predict gene expression patterns and regulatory elements with unprecedented accuracy.

Protein Semantics

Applying semantic analysis techniques to protein structures to understand their functional relationships and evolutionary history.

Biological Machine Translation

Creating neural networks that can translate between human language descriptions of biological processes and their molecular implementations.

Cellular Communication

Modeling cell signaling pathways as information networks to decode the "language" cells use to coordinate complex behaviors.

Neural Code Deciphering

Investigating parallels between linguistic processing in the brain and information encoding in neural circuits.

Pathogen Linguistics

Analyzing viral and bacterial genomes as "foreign languages" to predict mutations and develop countermeasures.
